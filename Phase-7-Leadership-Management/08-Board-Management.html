Board Management

Introduction

If you raise funding or bring on advisors, you'll have a board of directors. Managing your board effectively is critical for getting support when you need it, avoiding interference when you don't, and maintaining a productive relationship with your investors and advisors. This chapter teaches you how to build and manage a board that helps CoodLoom succeed.

Understanding Boards

What Is a Board of Directors?

Legal Definition: A group of people elected to represent shareholders and oversee management of the company. Practical Definition (For startups): A group that:

  • Provides guidance and advice
  • Holds CEO accountable
  • Makes major decisions (financings, CEO changes, exits)
  • Opens doors and connections
  • Supports in tough times

Board Responsibilities

Fiduciary Duties: Duty of Care:

Board must:
                        
    
                        
  • Stay informed about company
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Make informed decisions
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Ask questions and challenge assumptions
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Attend meetings and prepare
    • 
                        
                        

                        
If they don't: Could be liable for negligence
Duty of Loyalty:

Board must:
                        
    
                        
  • Act in company's best interest
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Avoid conflicts of interest
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Not self-deal
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Maintain confidentiality
    • 
                        
                        

                        
If they don't: Could be liable for breach of fiduciary duty

  • Board composition changes
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Company sale or exit

Often Approves (Depends on documents):

- Large contracts (>$X amount)

You Decide (Day-to-day):

Choosing Board Members

What to Look For: For Investor Board Member:

✓ Senior partner (not junior)
                        
✓ Startup operating experience ✓ Pattern recognition in your space ✓ Strong network ✓ Available and responsive ✓ Reputation for being founder-friendly ✓ Chemistry with you Questions to ask:

                        
    
                        
  • "How many boards are you on?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
(Ideal: 3-5, not 10+)

                        
    
                        
  • "How often are you available between meetings?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "Tell me about a portfolio company that struggled. How did you help?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "Can I talk to 3 CEOs you work with?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Check references!
For Independent Board Member:

✓ Operated at scale you aspire to
                        
✓ Deep expertise in area you need (sales, product, etc.) ✓ Genuinely interested in helping ✓ Available (not too busy) ✓ Diverse perspective from founders/investors ✓ Well-connected Red flags: ✗ Professional board member (too many boards) ✗ Looking for salary (should be equity only, early stage) ✗ Ego-driven (wants to run the company) ✗ Can't commit meaningful time

    •

    •

Board Observer:

- Attends meetings
                        
    
                        
  • No voting rights
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • No fiduciary duty
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Advisory role
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Usually:

                        
    
                        
  • Non-lead investors
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Future investors (trial)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Advisors
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Sometimes: Co-founder who stepped back

Give observer seat to:

  • Potential future investor
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Advisor you want feedback from

Don't give to:

                        
    
                        
  • Everyone who asks
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Competitors
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • People who can't maintain confidentiality
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Keep observer count low (1-2 max).

Compensation:

Your Role as Presenter: Do:

✓ Start on time
                        
✓ Follow the agenda ✓ Present confidently (you're the expert) ✓ Be honest about challenges ✓ Ask for specific help ✓ Listen to advice (even if you disagree) ✓ Take notes on action items ✓ Thank them for their time
Don't:

✗ Read slides word-for-word
                        
✗ Hide problems ✗ Get defensive when questioned ✗ Go way over time ✗ Skip the hard topics ✗ Let board members dominate/talk over you ✗ Leave without clear next steps
Handling Tough Questions:

When you don't know the answer:
                        
"That's a great question. I don't have that data with me, but I'll get it to you by [specific date]." When you disagree: "I hear your concern. Here's how I'm thinking about it: [reasoning]. I'm open to other perspectives." When criticized: "Thank you for that feedback. Let me think about how to address that. Can we discuss this more after the meeting?" Never:

                        
    
                        
  • Make up answers
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Get defensive or argumentative
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Dismiss their concern

  • More candid conversations
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Build relationship
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Get coaching

Managing Conflicts

When Board Members Disagree: Among themselves:

Your role: Facilitator
                        
    
                        
  • Let them debate
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Don't take sides immediately
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Synthesize different views
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Make the final call (on operational matters)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Get alignment on path forward

With you:

Your approach:
                        
    
                        
  • Listen fully to their concern
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Explain your reasoning
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Find common ground
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Compromise if possible
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Escalate if needed (full board vote)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Remember: They have perspective you don't. But you have context they don't. Both are valuable.
Micromanaging board member:

Politely push back:
                        
"I appreciate your input on [topic]. I've got this handled. Where I really need your help is [other topic]." Set boundaries: "I'll keep you updated on this monthly. Let's focus our 1-on-1 time on strategic topics where your experience is most valuable." If continues: Private conversation: "I value your input, but I need space to execute. Let's discuss how we can work together most effectively."

The Executive Session

What It Is: 15-30 minutes at end of board meeting, board without CEO. Purpose:

  • Discuss CEO performance
  • Raise concerns
  • Discuss sensitive topics
  • Ensure CEO is getting support needed

Your Response: Don't:

✗ Feel threatened or paranoid
                        
✗ Try to avoid it ✗ Get defensive when they give feedback
Do:

✓ Welcome it (shows board is engaged)
                        
✓ Ask for feedback after ✓ Listen openly ✓ Act on constructive input
What they might discuss:

- Is CEO performing well?
                        
    
                        
  • Does CEO need more support/coaching?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Are we concerned about anything?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • What should we communicate to CEO?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Are there blind spots?

After Executive Session:

Board chair typically shares:
                        
    
                        
  • Overall sentiment (positive/concerns)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Specific feedback
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Areas where they can help more
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Take it seriously, but not personally.

Fundraising and Board Composition

How Fundraising Affects Board

Typical terms:

    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "[Y] designated by investors"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "[Z] designated by founders"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "[W] independent, mutually agreed"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Example: 5-person board:

                        
    
                        
  • 2 designated by investors
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • 2 designated by founders
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • 1 independent (both sides agree)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Negotiate these terms carefully!
Board Rights to Negotiate: Try to maintain:

✓ Founder majority (or tied with independent)
                        
✓ Right to choose independent board members ✓ Ability to expand board (to add more independents) ✓ Board observer limits (not unlimited)
Watch out for:

✗ Investor majority control
                        
✗ Investor unilateral right to choose independents ✗ Excessive board observers ✗ Required board approval for all spending over $X (if too low)

Adding New Board Members

When Series A investor joins:

Options:
                        
1. Expand board (3 people → 4 people)

                        
    
                        
  • Previous seed investor stays
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Series A investor joins
    • 
                        
                        

                        
2. Replace seed investor

                        
    
                        
  • Seed investor becomes observer
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Series A investor takes board seat
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Decision factors:

                        
    
                        
  • How helpful is seed investor?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Do they want to stay?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • What's optimal board size?

Adding Independent:

Process:
                        
1. Define what expertise you need 2. Get recommendations (from investors, network) 3. Interview candidates (like hiring) 4. Check references thoroughly 5. Propose to board for approval 6. Formalize with offer letter Compensation (typical):

                        
    
                        
  • 0.25-0.5% equity (4 year vest, 1 year cliff)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • No cash comp (early stage)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • D&O insurance coverage

Board Best Practices

Communication Excellence

Be Transparent:

Share the good and the bad.
                        
Bad news doesn't age well. If something's wrong:

                        
    
                        
  • Tell them immediately
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Explain what you're doing
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Ask for help if needed
    • 
                        
                        

                        
They've seen it before. They can help. Hiding it makes it worse.
No Surprises:

Rule: Board should never be surprised in a board meeting.
                        
If big news (good or bad):

                        
    
                        
  • Email them before the meeting
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Or call if very significant
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Give them time to process
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Surprises → Loss of trust

Meeting Efficiency

Respect Their Time:

- Start exactly on time (even if someone late)
                        
    
                        
  • Send materials 3+ days before
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Keep to agenda and timeline
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • End on time (or early)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Don't waste time on stuff that could be email

Focus on Value:

Less time on:
                        
    
                        
  • Detailed operational updates (send these beforehand)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Reporting past results (in the deck)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
More time on:

                        
    
                        
  • Strategic discussions
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Problem-solving sessions
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Getting their expertise on key decisions
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Areas where they can help

Building Relationships

Beyond Board Meetings:

Invest in relationships:
                        
    
                        
  • Coffee or lunch one-on-one
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Introduce them to team members
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Share personal updates
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Remember details about their lives
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Ask about their portfolio/work
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Strong relationship = Better advice, more help, more support in tough times
Include Them in Wins:

When good news:
                        
    
                        
  • Customer win they helped with → Thank them publicly
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Hire they introduced → Acknowledge their help
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Advice that worked → Tell them the outcome
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Recognition builds engagement.

Warning Signs

Red Flags in Board Members

Investor Board Member Red Flags:

🚩 Rarely available between meetings
                        
🚩 Doesn't respond to emails for weeks 🚩 Tries to micromanage operations 🚩 Undermines CEO authority with team 🚩 Breaches confidentiality 🚩 Focuses only on near-term metrics 🚩 Pushes for premature exit 🚩 Doesn't prepare for meetings
Independent Board Member Red Flags:

🚩 Consistently misses meetings
                        
🚩 Doesn't add value in discussions 🚩 Self-promotional 🚩 On too many other boards (no time) 🚩 Conflicted (advising competitors)
What to Do:

1. Direct conversation
                        
"I'm not getting the value I expected from our board relationship. Here's what would be more helpful..." 2. If no improvement "I don't think this is the right fit. Let's discuss transition." 3. Remove from board

                        
    
                        
  • Replace with more helpful member
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Better to have smaller, engaged board

When Board Becomes a Problem

Board Dysfunction Signs:

- Constant conflict between members
                        
    
                        
  • Micromanaging day-to-day operations
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Every decision requires board approval
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Board split on strategy (no resolution)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Lack of trust in CEO
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Information leaks

How to Address:

1. Name the issue
                        
Bring up in board meeting or individual conversations 2. Propose solutions

                        
    
                        
  • Better communication norms
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Clearer decision rights
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • More/less frequent meetings
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Conflict resolution process
    • 
                        
                        

                        
3. Get external help

                        
    
                        
  • Board coach/facilitator
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Lawyer (if serious)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
4. Last resort

                        
    
                        
  • Remove problematic member (if possible)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • May need to leave company (extreme cases)

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Board is there to help you succeed: Use them as resources
  • ✓ Transparency builds trust: Share bad news early and often
  • ✓ No surprises: Keep them informed between meetings
  • ✓ Prepare thoroughly: Send materials early, run efficient meetings
  • ✓ Leverage their expertise: Ask for specific help
  • ✓ Build relationships: Beyond formal meetings
  • ✓ Respect their time: Start on time, end on time, focus on value
  • ✓ Manage actively: Don't let board manage you
  • ✓ Set boundaries: Pushback on micromanagement
  • ✓ Evolve composition: Board should change as company grows
