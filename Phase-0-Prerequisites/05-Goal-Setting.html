Goal Setting: Defining Your Vision for CoodLoom

Introduction

You've learned business fundamentals, assessed your readiness, and understood the industry. Now it's time to define exactly where you want to take CoodLoom. Clear goals are the difference between wandering and purposeful progress. This chapter will help you:

  • Define your long-term vision
  • Set specific, measurable goals
  • Create actionable milestones
  • Build accountability systems
  • Align with your co-founder on direction

Part 1: Vision Definition

Your "Why" - The Foundation

Before setting goals, you need to understand your deeper purpose. Reflection Exercise: Complete these statements: 1. I'm building CoodLoom because...

2. The problem I want to solve is...

3. Success for me means...

4. In 10 years, I want to...

5. The impact I want to make is...

Vision Statement for CoodLoom

A vision statement answers: "What do we aspire to become?" Template:

"[Company] aspires to be [position in market] by [unique approach],
                        
enabling [target customers] to [transformation/benefit], ultimately creating [broader impact]."
Examples from Successful Companies: Shopify: "Make commerce better for everyone, so businesses can focus on what they do best: building and selling their products." Square: "We started Square to make commerce easy and accessible to everyone." Microsoft (early): "A computer on every desk and in every home." Your CoodLoom Vision Statement (Draft):

Questions to Test Your Vision:

  • Is it inspiring? (Does it excite you?)
  • Is it clear? (Can anyone understand it?)
  • Is it ambitious? (Does it push boundaries?)
  • Is it focused? (Does it guide decisions?)
  • Is it enduring? (Will it last 10+ years?)

Refinement: Share with 5 people and ask: 1. What do you think CoodLoom does? 2. Who do you think it's for? 3. Does this sound exciting/valuable? Final Vision Statement:

Mission Statement for CoodLoom

A mission statement answers: "What do we do, for whom, and how?" Template:

"[Company] [what you do] for [target customer]
                        
through [how you do it differently]."
Examples: Toast: "We build software that helps restaurants operate their business, serve their guests, and grow their brands." Clover: "We help businesses run, grow, and make smarter decisions through our point-of-sale and business management platform." Your CoodLoom Mission Statement:

Part 2: Long-Term Goals (3-10 Years)

The "Big Hairy Audacious Goal" (BHAG)

A BHAG is your 10-year moonshot goal - ambitious but achievable. Examples:

  • "Become the #1 POS for [specific industry] in [region]"
  • "Reach $100M in revenue"
  • "Serve 10,000+ small businesses"
  • "Build a $500M+ company"
  • "Exit for $50M+"

Your 10-Year BHAG for CoodLoom:

Make it SMART:

  • Specific: Exactly what you want to achieve
  • Measurable: How you'll know you achieved it
  • Achievable: Ambitious but realistic
  • Relevant: Aligned with your vision
  • Time-bound: 10-year target

Part 4: Quarterly Goals (OKRs)

Understanding OKRs

OKR = Objectives and Key Results Objective: What you want to achieve (qualitative, inspirational) Key Results: How you'll measure success (quantitative, specific) Example:

  • Objective: Dominate the coffee shop POS market in Seattle
  • Key Result 1: Sign 25 Seattle coffee shops
  • Key Result 2: Achieve 4.8+ star average review score
  • Key Result 3: Capture 15% of Seattle coffee shop market

Part 9: Dealing with Goal Challenges

When You're Off Track

It Will Happen. Here's how to handle it: Step 1: Diagnose Why

  • Was the goal unrealistic?
  • Did priorities change?
  • Were there external factors?
  • Did I not execute?
  • Did I lack skills/resources?

Step 2: Decide What to Do

  • Adjust the goal: If unrealistic
  • Increase effort: If execution issue
  • Change approach: If strategy wrong
  • Deprioritize: If no longer relevant
  • Get help: If skill/resource gap

Step 3: Communicate

  • Tell co-founder immediately
  • Update stakeholders
  • Revise plans
  • Document learnings

Step 4: Learn

  • What assumption was wrong?
  • What did I underestimate?
  • What will I do differently?
  • How can I prevent this?

When Goals Conflict

Example: "Grow fast" vs "Stay profitable" Resolution Process: 1. Identify the conflict explicitly 2. Understand the trade-offs 3. Refer back to vision/values 4. Make intentional choice 5. Accept consequences 6. Revisit periodically Your Values Dictate Priorities:

  • If you value sustainability Profit may win
  • If you value impact Growth may win
  • If you value control Bootstrapping may win

Pivoting vs Persisting

When to Consider Changing Goals:

  • New information invalidates assumption
  • Market fundamentally changes
  • Better opportunity emerges
  • Current path clearly not working

When to Keep Pushing:

  • Goal is still aligned with vision
  • Making progress (even if slow)
  • Haven't given it enough time
  • Obstacles are temporary

Red Flags (Time to pivot):

  • No customer traction after 6+ months serious effort
  • Consistent feedback that you're solving wrong problem
  • Market is disappearing
  • Unit economics don't work and can't be fixed
  • Team/co-founder fundamentally misaligned
