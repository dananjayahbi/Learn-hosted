Sales Team Building

Introduction

You can't scale a SaaS company on founder-led sales forever. At some point, you need to build a sales team that can replicate your success. This chapter covers how to hire, train, and manage a high-performing sales organization that drives predictable revenue growth.

Key Concepts

1. The Sales Hiring Journey

Stage 1: Founder-Led Sales (0-10 customers)

  • Founders do all selling
  • Learn what works
  • Understand customer objections
  • Validate pricing
  • Don't hire yet!

Stage 2: First Sales Hire (10-30 customers)

  • Hire one sales rep
  • Train intensively
  • Work alongside them
  • Document process
  • Prove it's repeatable

Stage 3: Small Team (30-100 customers)

  • Hire 2-5 reps
  • Designate sales manager (maybe you)
  • Create playbooks
  • Establish metrics
  • Build sales operations

Stage 4: Scaled Team (100+ customers)

  • Dedicated sales leader (VP Sales)
  • Multiple teams (SDRs, AEs, CSMs)
  • Specialized roles
  • Formal training program
  • Sales ops team

Common Mistake: Hiring too early. Wait until you have 10+ customers from founder-led sales.

2. Sales Team Structure

For B2B SaaS: Small Team (1-5 people):

CEO (closes deals)
                        
├── Sales Rep 1 (full-cycle: prospect to close) ├── Sales Rep 2 (full-cycle: prospect to close) └── Sales Rep 3 (full-cycle: prospect to close)
Medium Team (5-15 people):

VP Sales
                        
├── SDR Team (3-5 people) │   ├── SDR Manager │   └── SDRs (prospecting, qualifying) ├── AE Team (3-5 people) │   ├── AE Manager │   └── Account Executives (demos, closing) └── Sales Ops (1 person) └── CRM, reporting, tools
Large Team (15+ people):

CRO (Chief Revenue Officer)
                        
├── VP Sales Development │   └── SDR Teams (by region/segment) ├── VP Sales │   └── AE Teams (by region/segment) ├── VP Customer Success │   └── CSM Teams └── VP Sales Operations └── Ops, enablement, analytics

3. Sales Roles Defined

SDR (Sales Development Rep)

  • Focus: Top of funnel (prospecting, qualifying)
  • Activities: Cold outreach, lead qualification, booking meetings
  • Quota: 20-40 qualified meetings/month
  • Compensation: $40K-60K base + $40K-60K variable
  • Career path: Promote to AE after 12-18 months

AE (Account Executive)

  • Focus: Mid to bottom funnel (demos, closing)
  • Activities: Product demos, proposals, negotiation, closing
  • Quota: $500K-$1M ARR/year (depends on product)
  • Compensation: $60K-80K base + $60K-80K variable
  • Career path: Senior AE, Team Lead, Manager

CSM (Customer Success Manager)

  • Focus: Retention and expansion
  • Activities: Onboarding, adoption, upsells, renewals
  • Quota: <5% churn, 20% expansion
  • Compensation: $50K-70K base + $30K-50K variable

Sales Manager

  • Focus: Team performance
  • Activities: Coaching, hiring, forecasting, strategy
  • Manages: 5-8 reps
  • Compensation: $80K-120K base + $40K-80K variable

VP Sales

  • Focus: Overall sales strategy and execution
  • Activities: Team building, process, forecasting, board updates
  • Manages: Sales managers and key accounts
  • Compensation: $120K-180K base + $80K-150K variable + equity

Hiring Your First Sales Rep

When to Hire

Ready to Hire When:

  • ✓ 10+ customers from founder-led sales
  • ✓ Repeatable sales process documented
  • ✓ Product is stable (not constantly breaking)
  • ✓ Clear ideal customer profile
  • ✓ Customer success process exists
  • ✓ Can afford 6-12 months to ramp
  • ✓ You have time to train them

Not Ready When:

  • ✗ No consistent founder-led sales
  • ✗ Product still finding PMF
  • ✗ High churn (>5% monthly)
  • ✗ No sales process documented
  • ✗ Can't afford 6-month ramp time

Ideal First Sales Rep Profile

Background:

  • 2-5 years B2B sales experience
  • SaaS experience preferred (but not required)
  • Sold to similar customer (restaurant industry for CoodLoom)
  • Hungry and coachable
  • Track record of quota achievement

Skills:

  • ✓ Self-starter (you can't babysit)
  • ✓ Organized (manages own pipeline)
  • ✓ Curious (asks good questions)
  • ✓ Resilient (handles rejection)
  • ✓ Consultative (not pushy)
  • ✓ Tech-savvy (learns software quickly)

Red Flags:

  • ✗ Too senior (VP wanting AE role)
  • ✗ Job hopper (3+ jobs in 2 years)
  • ✗ Blames others for failures
  • ✗ Can't explain their sales process
  • ✗ Needs extensive hand-holding
  • ✗ Arrogant or pushy

Interview Process

Stage 1: Phone Screen (30 min)

  • Recruiter or founder
  • Basic qualification
  • Understand background
  • Gauge interest
  • Screen out obviously wrong fits

Stage 2: Video Interview (60 min)

  • Hiring manager (probably you)
  • Deeper dive on experience
  • Sales methodology questions
  • Situational questions
  • Culture fit assessment

Sample Questions:

  • "Walk me through your current sales process, start to finish."
  • "Tell me about your best deal. What made it successful?"
  • "Tell me about a deal you lost. What happened?"
  • "How do you handle objections about price?"
  • "How do you prioritize your time?"
  • "What motivates you?"

Stage 3: Role Play (45 min)

  • Have them sell you something
  • Give them your product to demo
  • See how they think on feet
  • Assess communication skills
  • Check for coachability

Scenarios:

  • "Sell me this pen"
  • "Demo our POS to me (I'm a restaurant owner)"
  • "Handle this objection: 'Too expensive'"

Stage 4: Reference Checks

  • Call 2-3 references
  • Ask about quota attainment
  • Ask about strengths/weaknesses
  • Verify claims from interviews

Stage 5: Offer

  • Clear comp structure
  • Expectations and quotas
  • Onboarding plan
  • Start date

Total Timeline: 2-3 weeks

Compensation Structure

Base + Variable Model: For CoodLoom AE (Full-Cycle Rep):

  • Base: $60,000/year
  • Variable: $60,000/year (on-target earnings = OTE)
  • Total OTE: $120,000/year
  • Quota: $600,000 ARR/year
  • Commission: 10% of ARR (first year revenue)

Ramp Period:

  • Month 1-3: No quota (learning)
  • Month 4-6: 50% quota
  • Month 7-9: 75% quota
  • Month 10+: 100% quota

Accelerators:

  • 100-110% of quota: 1x commission rate
  • 110-125% of quota: 1.25x commission rate
  • 125%+ of quota: 1.5x commission rate

Example Earnings:

  • 100% quota ($600K sold): $60K base + $60K variable = $120K
  • 120% quota ($720K sold): $60K base + $72K variable = $132K
  • 150% quota ($900K sold): $60K base + $105K variable = $165K

4. Sales Process Stages Stage 1: Prospecting

  • Source: Cold outreach, inbound leads, referrals
  • Activity: Initial contact
  • Goal: Book discovery call
  • Duration: Ongoing

Stage 2: Discovery Call

  • Duration: 30 minutes
  • Goal: Understand needs, qualify fit
  • Outcome: Book demo or disqualify
  • Success rate: 60% → Demo

Stage 3: Product Demo

  • Duration: 45-60 minutes
  • Goal: Show value, excitement
  • Outcome: Proposal or follow-up
  • Success rate: 50% → Proposal

Stage 4: Proposal

  • Duration: Async + 30 min follow-up
  • Goal: Present pricing, ROI
  • Outcome: Negotiation or close
  • Success rate: 40% → Negotiation

Stage 5: Negotiation

  • Duration: 1-2 weeks
  • Goal: Address concerns, finalize terms
  • Outcome: Closed-won or closed-lost
  • Success rate: 70% → Close

Overall: 100 prospects → 60 discoveries → 30 demos → 12 proposals → 8 closes 5. Qualifying Framework: BANT Budget: Can they afford it?

  • "What's your current POS costing you?"
  • "What budget have you allocated for this?"

Authority: Can they decide?

  • "Who else is involved in this decision?"
  • "What's your decision-making process?"

Need: Do they have the problem?

  • "What challenges are you facing with current POS?"
  • "What would success look like?"

Timeline: When will they buy?

  • "When are you looking to make a change?"
  • "What's driving the timeline?"

Qualified lead: Yes to all four. Otherwise, nurture or disqualify. 6. Demo Script Intro (5 min):

  • Agenda overview
  • Learn about their restaurant
  • Confirm their pain points

Discovery (10 min):

  • "Tell me about your current process"
  • "What works well?"
  • "What's frustrating?"
  • "What would ideal solution look like?"

Demo (30 min):

  • Address their specific pain points
  • Show relevant features
  • Use their menu/data if possible
  • Handle objections
  • Create "aha" moments

Wrap-up (5 min):

  • Recap value shown
  • Answer questions
  • Next steps (proposal, trial, etc.)
  • Book follow-up

7. Common Objections & Responses "Too expensive"

  • "I understand cost is important. Let's look at ROI..."
  • "Compared to [current system], we're actually [cheaper/same] when you consider..."
  • "What if we could show you'll save [X hours/week] or increase sales by [Y%]?"

"We're happy with current system"

  • "That's great to hear. What do you like most about it?"
  • "If you could wave a magic wand and improve one thing, what would it be?"
  • "Most of our customers were happy with their old system too, until they saw..."

"Need to think about it"

  • "Totally understand. What specifically do you need to think about?"
  • "What questions can I answer to help you make a decision?"
  • "What's your timeline for making a change?"

"Not the right time"

  • "I understand timing is important. What's driving that timeline?"
  • "What would need to change for it to be the right time?"
  • "What if we could get you started with minimal disruption?"

Training Your Sales Team

30-60-90 Day Onboarding Plan

Days 1-30: Learn

  • Week 1: Product training
    • Use the product extensively
    • Know every feature
    • Complete mock transactions
    • Break things and learn limits
  • Week 2: Market & customer training
    • Read all case studies
    • Listen to sales call recordings
    • Shadow customer support
    • Learn about competitors
  • Week 3: Sales process training
    • Review playbook
    • Practice demos with team
    • Role-play objection handling
    • Set up CRM and tools
  • Week 4: First customer interactions
    • Join live sales calls (observe)
    • Attend customer meetings
    • Start prospecting activities
    • Book first solo discovery call

Goals:

  • Complete product certification
  • Pass sales process quiz
  • Shadow 10+ sales calls
  • Build pipeline of 20 prospects

Days 31-60: Practice

  • Lead discovery calls (you shadow)
  • Conduct demos (you shadow)
  • Handle objections (you intervene if needed)
  • Send proposals (you review)
  • Close first deal (with your help)

Goals:

  • 10 discovery calls
  • 5 demos
  • 2 proposals
  • 1 closed deal (team celebration!)

Days 61-90: Perform

  • Full autonomy on deals
  • You spot-check, don't shadow
  • Close 2-3 deals
  • Hit 50% of quota

Goals:

  • 20 discovery calls
  • 10 demos
  • 4 proposals
  • 2-3 closed deals

Ongoing Training

Forecasting

Types of Forecasts: Pipeline Forecast:

  • Stage-weighted pipeline value
  • Example: $100K in demo stage (40% likely) = $40K forecast

Historical Forecast:

  • Based on past performance
  • Example: Historically close $50K/month, forecast $50K

Bottoms-Up Forecast:

  • Reps forecast their deals
  • Roll up to team forecast

Top-Down Forecast:

  • Leadership sets targets
  • Allocate to teams

Best Practice: Use multiple methods, compare, adjust. Forecast Meetings:

  • Weekly: Review pipeline changes
  • Monthly: Update forecast for quarter
  • Quarterly: Set next quarter targets

Hiring Sales Leadership

When to Hire VP Sales

Hire VP Sales when:

  • 5-10 sales reps
  • You (CEO/founder) spending 50%+ on sales
  • Need strategic sales expertise
  • Raising significant capital
  • Entering new phase of growth

Too Early:

  • <3 sales reps
  • Process not repeatable
  • You don't know what works yet

VP Sales Profile: Experience:

  • 10+ years sales experience
  • 5+ years sales leadership
  • Scaled team from X to Y
  • Industry or segment expertise
  • Built processes and systems

Skills:

  • Strategic thinker
  • Team builder
  • Process-oriented
  • Data-driven
  • Excellent communicator
  • Culture fit

Compensation:

  • Base: $120K-180K
  • Variable: $80K-150K
  • Equity: 0.5-1.5% (depending on stage)
  • Total: $200K-330K OTE

Common Sales Mistakes

1. Hiring Too Senior Too Early

Mistake: Hiring VP Sales as first sales hire Problem: Too expensive, wants to manage not sell Solution: Hire IC (individual contributor) first

2. No Onboarding Plan

Mistake: Throwing rep into calls immediately Problem: Rep fails, wastes time, costs money Solution: 30-60-90 day onboarding plan

3. Unrealistic Quotas

Mistake: Setting quota at $1M when no rep has done $500K Problem: Demotivated team, high turnover Solution: Base quotas on actual performance data

4. Not Firing Fast Enough

Mistake: Keeping underperformers for 6+ months Problem: Costs money, hurts team morale Solution: PIP within 3 months, decision by 6 months

5. Neglecting Training

Mistake: Train once, then let reps figure it out Problem: Inconsistent results, skill gaps persist Solution: Ongoing weekly training and coaching

6. Poor CRM Hygiene

Mistake: Not enforcing CRM usage Problem: Can't forecast, lost opportunities Solution: Make CRM mandatory, part of performance review

7. Comp Plan Too Complex

Mistake: 10-page compensation plans Problem: Reps don't understand, gaming the system Solution: Simple, clear comp plans (1 page)

Case Studies

Case Study 1: Salesforce - Inside Sales Model

Challenge: Scale B2B SaaS sales Approach:

  • Pioneered inside sales for enterprise
  • SDR → AE → CSM model
  • Heavy process and training
  • Technology-enabled selling
  • Repeatable, scalable model

Results:

  • Scaled to thousands of reps
  • $31B+ revenue
  • Model copied by all SaaS

Key Lessons:

  • Inside sales can work for enterprise
  • Process + technology = scale
  • Invest in training
  • Specialize roles (SDR vs. AE)

Challenge: Scale with freemium model Approach:

  • Free CRM drives awareness
  • Product-qualified leads (PQLs)
  • Sales helps convert power users
  • Customer success drives expansion
  • Blended PLG + sales-led

Results:

  • 150K+ customers
  • Efficient scaling
  • Low CAC
  • High NRR

Key Lessons:

  • Product can drive leads
  • Sales focuses on conversion and expansion
  • Specialize by customer segment
  • Efficiency over headcount

Case Study 3: Gong - Elite Sales Org

Challenge: Build world-class sales team Approach:

  • Hire A+ talent
  • Extensive training (Gong University)
  • Use own product for coaching
  • Data-driven everything
  • Strong culture

Results:

  • 3,000+ customers
  • $200M+ ARR
  • 95%+ quota attainment
  • Industry-leading win rates

Key Lessons:

  • Invest in the best people
  • Training is never done
  • Use data to coach
  • Culture eats strategy

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Don't hire sales reps until you have 10+ founder-led sales
  • ✓ Hire hungry, coachable reps over experienced but complacent ones
  • ✓ Document your sales process before scaling
  • ✓ Invest in thorough 30-60-90 day onboarding
  • ✓ Keep compensation plans simple and transparent
  • ✓ Track both activity (leading) and results (lagging) metrics
  • ✓ Coach weekly, course-correct fast
  • ✓ Hire sales ops and VP Sales at the right time (not too early)
  • ✓ Build repeatable, scalable processes
  • ✓ Culture and training matter as much as talent
