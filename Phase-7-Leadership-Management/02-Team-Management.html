Team Management

Introduction

Your team is your most valuable asset. Great products are built by great teams, and great teams are built by great leaders. This chapter teaches you how to hire, develop, motivate, and retain exceptional people who will help you build CoodLoom into a thriving business.

Building Your First Team

The First 10 Hires Are Critical

Why they matter:

  • Set the culture and standards
  • Attract (or repel) future talent
  • Determine company DNA
  • Impact success disproportionately

For CoodLoom's First 10:

Hire #1-2: Co-founder (already done) + Technical lead
                        
Hire #3-4: Sales/Marketing leader + Customer success Hire #5-6: Senior developers Hire #7-8: Product/Design + Operations Hire #9-10: Account executives or additional engineers Each hire should be someone who:

                        
    
                        
  • Can work independently
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Raises the average talent level
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Fits the culture you want to build
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Brings skills you lack

Hiring Philosophy

Hire for Slope, Not Y-Intercept:

  • Y-Intercept: What they know today
  • Slope: How fast they learn and grow
  • For startups: Slope > Y-Intercept

The "Hell Yes" or "No" Rule:

  • If you're not absolutely sure, it's a no
  • Hiring mistakes are expensive (time, money, morale)
  • Empty seat better than wrong person
  • Be patient for the right people

Diversity Drives Innovation:

  • Different backgrounds = different perspectives
  • Cognitive diversity > demographic diversity (but both matter)
  • Avoid hiring people just like you
  • Build complementary skill sets

Motivating Your Team

Understanding Motivation

Intrinsic vs Extrinsic Motivation: Extrinsic (External):

  • Money and bonuses
  • Promotions and titles
  • Recognition and awards
  • Perks and benefits

Intrinsic (Internal):

  • Autonomy (control over work)
  • Mastery (getting better)
  • Purpose (meaningful work)
  • Belonging (part of something)

The Modern Approach: Extrinsic factors must be fair, but intrinsic factors drive sustained high performance.

SBI Model (Situation-Behavior-Impact):

Situation:
                        
"In yesterday's product meeting..." Behavior: "...you interrupted the designer three times while they were presenting..." Impact: "...which made them shut down and we didn't get their full input. The team needs to hear everyone's ideas to make good decisions." Then: "What happened from your perspective?" [Listen] "How could you handle this differently next time?"
Positive Feedback (Don't Skip This):

Situation:
                        
"When the customer called with the critical bug on Friday at 5pm..." Behavior: "...you stayed late, debugged it, pushed a fix, and personally called the customer to confirm it was resolved..." Impact: "...which saved the account and showed incredible ownership. That's exactly the kind of customer obsession we need. Thank you."

The Termination Conversation

Reasons for Termination:

  • Performance (after PIP and documented efforts)
  • Culture fit issues (toxic behavior)
  • Dishonesty or ethics violations
  • Company downsizing/pivot

How to Do It Right:

Preparation:
                        
    
                        
  • Consult with HR/lawyer if you have one
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Have documentation ready
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Decide on severance
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Plan the logistics
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Choose right time (early in week, early in day)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
The Meeting (10-15 minutes): 1. Be direct immediately: "I've made the difficult decision that today will be your last day." 2. Brief explanation (no debate): "We've discussed performance concerns over the past 60 days. Despite the support provided, we haven't seen the improvement needed." 3. Logistics: "Here's your severance package [X weeks pay]. Your access will be deactivated after this meeting. HR will cover COBRA and final pay details." 4. End kindly: "I know this is disappointing. I wish you the best in finding the right fit for your skills." What NOT to do:

                        
    
                        
  • Don't drag it out or soften the message
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Don't debate or argue
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Don't trash talk them
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Don't do it over email/Zoom (unless remote)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Don't do it right before weekend/holiday

After the Termination:

To the Team:
                        
    
                        
  • Announce the same day
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Be brief and respectful
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Don't trash talk the person
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Don't share inappropriate details
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Focus on moving forward
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Example: "I want to let you know that [Name] is no longer with the company. We wish them well. [Name] will be taking over their responsibilities. If you have questions, my door is open."

Scaling Your Management

From Manager to Manager of Managers

When to Promote to Leadership:

  • Team exceeds 7-8 direct reports
  • Clear leadership potential identified
  • Someone wants the growth
  • Company can support the structure

What Changes:

As Individual Contributor Manager:
                        
    
                        
  • Direct impact on work
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Clear feedback loops
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Close to the product
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Hands-on problem solving
    • 
                        
                        

                        
As Manager of Managers:

                        
    
                        
  • Indirect impact through others
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Delayed feedback loops
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Further from execution
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Coaching over doing

Supporting New Managers:

  • Management training
  • Regular coaching sessions
  • Share your learnings
  • Give them autonomy
  • Accept that they'll do it differently

Key Takeaways

  • Hire slow, fire fast: Getting the right people is your most important job
  • First 10 hires set culture: They define what "good" looks like
  • Onboarding determines success: Great onboarding = faster productivity and better retention
  • AMP drives motivation: Autonomy, Mastery, Purpose > money alone
  • 1:1s are sacred: Never cancel, always prepare, truly listen
  • Feedback is a gift: Specific, timely, constructive
  • PIPs are tools, not paperwork: Use them to genuinely help or to document
  • Difficult conversations don't age well: Address issues quickly and directly
  • Culture is what you do: Not what you say or put on walls
  • Delegate or drown: You can't scale if you're doing everything
