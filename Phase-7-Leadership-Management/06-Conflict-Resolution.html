Conflict Resolution

Introduction

Conflict is inevitable in any growing company. Different perspectives, competing priorities, personality clashes, and high-stakes decisions create friction. As CEO, your ability to navigate and resolve conflict determines whether your team becomes stronger or fractures. This chapter teaches you how to turn conflict into opportunity for growth.

Understanding Conflict

Conflict Is Not Always Bad

Healthy Conflict (Productive):

✓ Debate about product direction
                        
✓ Discussion about priorities ✓ Challenging assumptions ✓ Questioning decisions ✓ Different approaches to problems ✓ Constructive disagreement Result: Better decisions, innovation, growth
Unhealthy Conflict (Destructive):

✗ Personal attacks
                        
✗ Hidden agendas ✗ Passive aggression ✗ Undermining others ✗ Political games ✗ Avoiding necessary conversations Result: Toxicity, turnover, dysfunction
Your Goal: Encourage healthy conflict, eliminate unhealthy conflict.

Types of Conflict

1. Task Conflict (About work):

Example: "Should we build feature A or B first?"
                        
Status: Healthy and necessary Action: Encourage debate, make decision, move forward
2. Process Conflict (About how work gets done):

Example: "Should we use Agile or Kanban?"
                        
Status: Can be healthy, but limit time spent Action: Test approaches, decide based on results
3. Relationship Conflict (Personal):

Example: "I don't trust Alex" or "Sarah is always difficult"
                        
Status: Unhealthy, address immediately Action: Mediate, set expectations, monitor
4. Values Conflict (Core beliefs):

Example: "I believe we should prioritize profit, you believe 
                        
we should prioritize mission" Status: Serious, may be irreconcilable Action: Deep conversation, may need to part ways

    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Avoiding certain team members
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Decreased collaboration
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Complaints through back channels
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Body language changes (crossed arms, no eye contact)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Performance:

                        
    
                        
  • Missed deadlines
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Quality decline
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Increased errors
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Lack of coordination
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Duplicated work
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Cultural:

    •

    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "Us vs them" language
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Blame culture emerges

Your Response:

Don't ignore hoping it resolves itself. It won't.
                        
Within 24-48 hours: 1. Talk to each party separately 2. Understand both perspectives 3. Assess severity 4. Decide on approach 5. Take action

Step 2: Gather Information

Before Intervening: Talk to Each Party Privately:

Questions to Ask:
                        
    
                        
  • "Help me understand what's happening from your perspective"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "What's your relationship been like with [person]?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "What specifically has occurred that concerns you?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "How is this affecting your work?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "What would an ideal resolution look like?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "What have you tried already?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Listen for:

                        
    
                        
  • Specific incidents (not vague feelings)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Patterns over time
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Impact on work and team
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Their role in the conflict (not just blaming others)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Willingness to resolve

Consider Multiple Perspectives:

Talk to:
                        
    
                        
  • Both parties involved
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Others on the team (if appropriate)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Previous managers (if relevant)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • HR or advisors
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Avoid:

                        
    
                        
  • Taking sides before understanding fully
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Making assumptions
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Believing only one side
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Skipping this step

Step 3: Classify the Conflict

Type A: Simple Misunderstanding

Example: Engineer thought deadline was Friday, PM thought Thursday
                        
Root cause: Communication gap Solution: Clarify and move forward Time: 15 minutes
Type B: Different Working Styles

Example: Detail-oriented person vs big-picture person clashing
                        
Root cause: Personality/style differences Solution: Understand and adapt to each other Time: 1-2 conversations
Type C: Competing Priorities

Example: Sales wants feature now, Engineering says it takes 3 months
                        
Root cause: Different incentives/perspectives Solution: Align on company priorities, make trade-offs clear Time: 1-2 meetings + decision
Type D: Performance or Behavior Issue

Example: One person not pulling weight, causing frustration
                        
Root cause: Performance or professionalism problem Solution: Performance management, clear expectations Time: PIP or immediate action
Type E: Values Misalignment

Example: Fundamental disagreement about company direction or ethics
                        
Root cause: Core values don't match Solution: Deep conversation, may require departure Time: Multiple conversations, possibly permanent separation

    •

When:

    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Power imbalance
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • High stakes
    • 
                        
                        

                        
You actively mediate, set ground rules, guide discussion.
Decision-Making (Competing priorities):

When:

    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Both have valid points
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Someone needs to decide
    • 
                        
                        

                        
You listen to both sides, make the call, both commit.
Performance Management (Individual issue):

When:
                        
    
                        
  • One person is the problem
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Behavior or performance issues
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Affecting team negatively
    • 
                        
                        

                        
You address with individual, set expectations, follow up.
Separation (Irreconcilable):

When:

    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Toxic behavior continues
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Values fundamentally misaligned

You let one or both people go.

Step 5: Facilitate Resolution

The Mediation Meeting: Preparation (Day before):

1. Confirm both parties willing to meet
                        
2. Set expectations for the conversation 3. Schedule 60-90 minutes 4. Choose neutral location 5. Prepare your facilitation questions
Opening (5 minutes):

Your script:
                        
"Thank you both for being here. I know this is uncomfortable, but I believe we can work through this together. Here's how this will work: 1. Each of you will share your perspective (10 min each) 2. No interrupting while the other speaks 3. Then we'll discuss together 4. Goal is mutual understanding and path forward 5. I'm here to facilitate, not judge Ground rules:

                        
    
                        
  • Focus on behaviors and impact, not character
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Use 'I' statements, not 'you' accusations
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Listen to understand, not to respond
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Assume positive intent
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Commit to resolution
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Any questions before we start?"
Perspective Sharing (20 minutes):

"[Person A], please share your perspective. [Person B], 
                        
please just listen for now." [Person A shares - 10 minutes] "Thank you. [Person B], what did you hear [Person A] say?" [Check for understanding] "Now [Person B], please share your perspective." [Person B shares - 10 minutes] "Thank you. [Person A], what did you hear [Person B] say?" [Check for understanding]
Discussion (30 minutes):

Facilitate exploration:
                        
    
                        
  • "Where do you agree?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "What's the core issue from each perspective?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "What's the impact on your work? On the team?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "What do you each need from the other going forward?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "What are you willing to commit to?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Guide toward:

                        
    
                        
  • Understanding (not necessarily agreement)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Acknowledgment of impact
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Specific behavior changes
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Path forward

Resolution (10 minutes):

Document agreements:
                        
    
                        
  • "So I'm hearing you'll both commit to [X]"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "Is that accurate?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "[Person A] will [specific behavior]"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "[Person B] will [specific behavior]"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "We'll check in [timeframe]"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "If this continues, [consequences]"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Get verbal commitment from both.
Follow-up (5 minutes):

Send email same day:
                        
    
                        
  • Summary of discussion
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Agreements made
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Next steps
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Check-in date
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Thank them for engaging constructively.

Step 6: Monitor and Follow-up

Weekly Check-ins (First month):

Talk to each party separately:
                        
    
                        
  • "How's it going with [person]?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "Are you both following through on commitments?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "Any concerns?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "What's better? What's not?"
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Observe interactions in meetings, Slack, etc.
Monthly Check-ins (Months 2-3):

Less frequent but ongoing:

    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Recognize improvements
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Address any backsliding immediately

Success Indicators:

✓ Collaborating effectively
                        
✓ Communicating directly ✓ No complaints from others ✓ Work quality and speed normal ✓ Positive body language ✓ Constructive disagreement (healthy conflict)
Failure Indicators:

✗ Avoiding each other
                        
✗ Passive aggression continues ✗ Others complain about tension ✗ Work suffers ✗ Commitments not kept If after 30 days no improvement → Stronger action needed

Conflict with Customer

De-escalation Framework: Step 1: Acknowledge (Immediately)

"I understand you're frustrated. Thank you for bringing 
                        
this to our attention. I want to make this right." Don't:

                        
    
                        
  • Defend immediately
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Blame them
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Make excuses
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Ignore

Step 2: Listen (Fully)

"Tell me exactly what happened from your perspective."
                        
Let them vent without interrupting. Take notes. Ask clarifying questions. Confirm understanding.
Step 3: Apologize (If appropriate)

"I'm sorry this happened. That's not the experience we 
                        
want you to have." Even if it's not your fault: "I'm sorry you had this experience."
Step 4: Solve (Collaboratively)

"Here's what I can do: [options]"
                        
"What would make this right for you?" Find solution that works for both.
Step 5: Follow-through (Completely)

Do what you promised.
                        
Check back: "Did we resolve this?" Learn: "How do we prevent this?"
When to Stand Firm:

- Customer is abusive to team
                        
    
                        
  • Request is unreasonable/impossible
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Would set bad precedent
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • They're not right fit
    • 
                        
                        

                        
"I understand your frustration, but we're not able to [unreasonable request]. We can offer [reasonable alternative], or if this doesn't work for you, we understand if you need to find another solution."

Difficult Conversations

The Structure

Opening (30 seconds):

Be direct, don't soften:
                        
❌ "Hey, how's it going? Nice weather... So anyway..." ✓ "I wanted to talk about [specific issue]. Do you have 15 minutes?" Set tone: ❌ "Don't take this the wrong way, but..." ✓ "I value you and I want to address something directly."
Situation-Behavior-Impact (2-3 minutes):

Situation: "In yesterday's client call..."
                        
Behavior: "...you interrupted the client three times..." Impact: "...which made them defensive and we lost momentum." Not: "You're always unprofessional" (Character attack) "You need to be better" (Vague)
Listen (5 minutes):

"What happened from your perspective?"
                        
Actually listen. Don't:

                        
    
                        
  • Prepare your rebuttal
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Interrupt
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Dismiss their view
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Get defensive
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Acknowledge their perspective: "I hear that you felt [X]. Help me understand why..."
Discuss Forward (5 minutes):

"Going forward, what I need is [specific behavior]."
                        
"What support do you need from me?" "How will we prevent this?" Get commitment: "Can you commit to [specific action]?" Get verbal "yes."
Document (After):

Send email summarizing:
                        
    
                        
  • What you discussed
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Commitments made
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Next steps
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Check-in date
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Especially important for performance issues.

Common Difficult Conversations

Performance Below Expectations:

"I want to talk about your performance on [project]. 
                        
The expectation was [X], and the result was [Y]. Here's the gap: [specific examples] This is impacting: [team/customer/business] Going forward, I need you to [specific improvements] by [specific date]. I'll support you with [resources]. Can you commit to this?"
Behavioral Issue:

"I need to give you direct feedback. In [situation], 
                        
you [behavior], which resulted in [impact]. Our value is [relevant value], and this doesn't align. I need you to [alternative behavior] going forward. Can you commit to that?"
Compensation Discussion:

"I know you were hoping for [X] raise, and I'm able 
                        
to offer [Y]. Here's why: [Business context, market data, budget reality] Here's what would need to change for you to get to [X]: [Specific performance improvements] Let's create a plan to get you there."
Letting Someone Go:

"I've decided that today will be your last day with CoodLoom.
                        
We've discussed performance concerns over the past [time period], and despite the support we've provided, we haven't seen the improvement we need. Here's what happens next:

                        
    
                        
  • [Severance details]
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • [Benefits information]
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • [Equipment return]
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • [Reference policy]
    • 
                        
                        

                        
I wish you the best in finding the right fit." [Don't debate, don't drag out, be respectful]

Your Conflict Resolution Toolkit

Pre-Conflict (Prevention)

□ Clear roles and responsibilities
                        
□ Documented decision-making process □ Regular 1-on-1s □ Psychological safety established □ Feedback culture normalized □ Values clearly defined and lived □ Communication norms set □ Regular team retrospectives

During Conflict (Resolution)

□ Recognition checklist (warning signs)
                        
□ Information gathering template □ Conflict classification framework □ Mediation meeting script □ Resolution agreement template □ Follow-up schedule

Post-Conflict (Learning)

□ Conflict log (patterns over time)
                        
□ Root cause analysis □ Process improvements □ Team debriefs (when appropriate) □ Personal reflection

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Intervene early: Stage 1 tension is easier than Stage 4 war
  • ✓ Healthy conflict is good: Debate and disagreement drive better decisions
  • ✓ Address within 48 hours: Don't let conflicts fester
  • ✓ Listen to all sides: Don't rush to judgment
  • ✓ Focus on behavior: Not character or personality
  • ✓ Document everything: Especially for serious or recurring issues
  • ✓ Follow up religiously: Resolution requires monitoring
  • ✓ Build prevention systems: Psychological safety, regular check-ins, clear norms
  • ✓ Model directly: Have difficult conversations yourself, team learns from you
  • ✓ Know when to separate: Some conflicts aren't resolvable

Exercises

Exercise 1: Conflict Inventory

List current or recent conflicts:

  • What type were they?
  • How quickly did you address them?
  • What was the outcome?
  • What would you do differently?

Exercise 2: Practice Scripts

Write out scripts for 3 difficult conversations you need to have:

  • Use SBI model
  • Be specific
  • Focus on forward behavior

Practice out loud.

Exercise 3: Prevention Audit

Rate 1-10:

  • How psychologically safe is your team?
  • How quickly do you address tensions?
  • How clear are roles and decision rights?
  • How well do people give/receive feedback?

For each below 7: Create improvement plan.

Exercise 4: Mediation Practice

Role-play a mediation with a friend:

  • Set up scenario
  • Practice your facilitation
  • Get feedback on your approach

Exercise 5: Conflict Norms

Document "How we handle conflict at CoodLoom":

  • 5-7 specific norms
  • Share with team
  • Reference in conflicts
