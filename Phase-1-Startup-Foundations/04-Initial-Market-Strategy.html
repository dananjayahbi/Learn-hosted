Initial Market Strategy

Introduction

You have validated your idea (Chapter 1), structured your partnership (Chapter 2), and built your MVP (Chapter 3). Now it's time to get customers. This chapter teaches you how to leverage your existing customers, acquire new ones, and build sustainable growth momentum. What You'll Learn:

  • Converting existing users to paying customers
  • Finding and acquiring your first 10, 50, 100 customers
  • Pricing strategy and positioning
  • Sales process for POS systems
  • Marketing channels that work for B2B SaaS
  • Measuring and optimizing acquisition

The Pricing Migration Strategy

DON'T:

  • ‚ùå Suddenly charge market rates to friends
  • ‚ùå Apply same price to everyone
  • ‚ùå Apologize for charging

DO:

  • ‚úì Grandfather early supporters with special pricing
  • ‚úì Tiered approach based on relationship
  • ‚úì Communicate value, not just price
  • ‚úì Offer choice and flexibility

The Conversation Framework

Step 1: Set the Context (2-3 weeks before price change)

"Hey [Name],
                        
I wanted to give you a heads up about CoodLoom. We're officially launching as a real business and transitioning from beta to a professional product. This means better support, more features, and professional-grade reliability. It also means we'll be moving to standard pricing. Because you've been with us from the beginning, I wanted to talk to you personally about what this means for you. Can we grab coffee/hop on a call this week?"
Step 2: The Conversation Acknowledge the relationship: "You've been incredibly helpful in getting CoodLoom to where it is. Your feedback has been invaluable." Explain the transition: "We're now at a point where we're ready to serve customers at scale. That means better everything - more features, faster support, higher reliability." Present the pricing: "Our standard pricing is $[market rate]/month. But because you believed in us early, I'd like to offer you [special pricing]." Options to Offer: Option 1: Founder's Pricing (50% lifetime discount)

  • $[50% of market rate]/month
  • Lock in this rate forever
  • All future features included
  • Priority support
  • Your logo on our website (if you want)

Option 2: Legacy Pricing (Year 1 discount, gradual increase)

  • Year 1: $[30% of market]
  • Year 2: $[60% of market]
  • Year 3+: $[100% of market]
  • Gives you time to see the value

Option 3: Standard Pricing (No discount)

  • $[market rate]/month
  • All standard features
  • If you prefer to be treated like any other customer

Present with confidence: "Which option makes the most sense for you?" Listen to concerns: If they have concerns, address them:

  • "That's more than I'm paying now" ‚Üí Show value added
  • "I'm not sure it's worth it" ‚Üí Offer free trial extension
  • "Can I think about it?" ‚Üí "Of course, let's reconnect Friday"

Close: "Great, I'll send you the details in an email. Thanks for being part of the CoodLoom journey from the start!"

Pricing Psychology

Anchoring: Present the standard price first, then the special price. "Standard pricing is $200/month, but for you, $100/month." Value-based pricing: "Our customers typically save 10 hours per week and reduce errors by 30%. What would that be worth to your business?" Comparison pricing: "Square charges $60/month + 2.9% per transaction. Toast is $165/month. We're $150/month with lower transaction fees." Early Adopter Appeal: "You're getting the same product we'll be charging $200/month for, at half the price, forever. That's your reward for believing early."

Handling Objections

Objection 1: "It's too expensive" Response: "I understand. Let me share what other customers have found: In the first month alone, the time saved and error reduction typically pays for the system 3x over. Can we look at the ROI for your specific case?" Show the math:

  • Hours saved per week: X hours √ó $Y per hour = $Z/week
  • Reduced errors: $A per month
  • Faster checkout: Serve X more customers = $B/month
  • Total value: $Z+A+B = Way more than subscription cost

Objection 2: "I was getting it for free" Response: "You were helping us build the product. Now that it's a professional product with all the features and support, there's real value being delivered. The founder's pricing I'm offering is still 50% off what everyone else will pay. It's my way of saying thank you." Objection 3: "Let me think about it" Response: "Absolutely. While you're thinking, here's what I'd suggest: Let's continue with your current setup for the next 30 days. I'll send you some data on your usage and the value you're getting. Then let's reconnect. Sound good?" Follow-up in 7 days with usage report showing value. Objection 4: "I'll just use [free competitor]" Response: "That's totally fair. Let me ask - what would you miss most if you switched? [Listen] Those are exactly the features that our customers value most. Free tools often lack [mention those features]. But I respect your decision. If you ever want to come back, your founder's pricing will be waiting." Sometimes letting them go is the right move. Not every customer is the right customer.

Conversion Tracking

After 30 days, you should have: Target Conversions:

  • 80%+ of high-value customers ‚Üí Paid
  • 60%+ of medium-value customers ‚Üí Paid
  • Some low-value customers ‚Üí Paid or gracefully exit

  • Your pricing might be too high
  • Your product might not deliver enough value
  • Your communication might need work
  • You might need to improve the product first

Pricing Strategy Deep Dive

Pricing Model Options

Option 1: Flat Subscription

  • $X per month
  • Unlimited users, transactions, features
  • Pros: Simple, predictable
  • Cons: Leaves money on table

Option 2: Tiered Pricing

  • Starter: $X (basic features)
  • Pro: $Y (advanced features)
  • Enterprise: $Z (custom)
  • Pros: Captures more value
  • Cons: More complex

Option 3: Per-Location Pricing

  • $X per location per month
  • Pros: Scales with customer size
  • Cons: Limits small business appeal

Option 4: Transaction-Based

  • $X per month + $Y per transaction
  • Pros: Aligns with customer usage
  • Cons: Unpredictable revenue

Option 5: Hybrid

  • Base: $X/month
  • Add-ons: $Y each
  • Pros: Flexibility
  • Cons: Complexity

Recommendation for CoodLoom: Start with Tiered (Option 2) Example Tier Structure: Starter - $99/month:

  • 1 location
  • Up to 3 users
  • Core POS features
  • Email support
  • Basic reports
  • Target: Small single-location businesses

Professional - $199/month:

  • Up to 3 locations
  • Unlimited users
  • Advanced reports
  • Priority support
  • Inventory management
  • Loyalty program
  • API access
  • Target: Growing businesses

Enterprise - Custom:

  • Unlimited locations
  • White-label option
  • Dedicated account manager
  • Custom integrations
  • SLA guarantee
  • Target: Multi-location chains

Pricing Psychology

Charm Pricing:

  • $99 vs $100 (feels cheaper)
  • $199 vs $200

Anchoring:

  • Show highest price first
  • Makes middle tier seem reasonable

Decoy Pricing:

  • Make middle tier obvious choice
  • Starter missing key features
  • Enterprise too expensive for most
  • Result: 70% choose Professional

Annual Discount:

  • Monthly: $199/month
  • Annual: $1,990/year (save $398)
  • Discount: 16-17% off
  • Benefit to you: Cash upfront, better retention

Price Testing

How to Test: 1. Start with initial pricing 2. After 20 customers, analyze willingness to pay 3. Adjust prices for next cohort 4. Grandfather existing customers 5. Repeat until optimal Signs Your Pricing is Too Low:

  • Everyone says yes immediately
  • No one negotiates
  • No price objections
  • High conversion but low revenue

Signs Your Pricing is Too High:

  • Low conversion rate
  • "Too expensive" is #1 objection
  • Competitors winning on price
  • Long sales cycles

Optimal Pricing:

  • 40-60% conversion rate
  • Some price objections (but not all)
  • Comparable to competitors
  • Room for growth

Handling Price Objections

Objection: "Competitor X is cheaper" Response: "That's true. Let me explain why CoodLoom costs more: 1. [Feature/benefit competitor lacks] 2. [Superior support/reliability] 3. [Specific value delivery] Most customers find that the additional cost is made up within the first month through [specific benefit]. Can I show you the ROI calculation for your specific case?" Objection: "Can you give me a discount?" Response: "I appreciate you asking. Our pricing is based on the value we deliver. That said, I can offer [annual discount / additional features / extended trial]. Would that work?" Never discount more than 10-20% without getting something in return:

  • Annual commitment
  • Case study permission
  • Referrals
  • Logo usage
  • Testimonial

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Trying Every Channel

Problem: Spread too thin, nothing works Solution: Pick 2-3 channels, master them

Mistake 2: Ignoring Existing Customers

Problem: Chase new customers, lose old ones Solution: Retention > Acquisition (cheaper too)

Mistake 3: Underpricing

Problem: Can't sustain business Solution: Charge what you're worth

Mistake 4: No Follow-Up System

Problem: Leads fall through cracks Solution: CRM + automated sequences

Mistake 5: Poor Qualification

Problem: Waste time on bad-fit customers Solution: Qualify early, focus on ICP

Mistake 6: Selling Features, Not Value

Problem: Customers don't care about features Solution: Sell outcomes (time saved, money made)

Mistake 7: No Metrics

Problem: Don't know what's working Solution: Track everything

Mistake 8: Giving Up Too Soon

Problem: Each channel needs time Solution: Give 3-6 months before deciding
