Contracts & Agreements: Legal Documents for CoodLoom

Introduction

Contracts are the foundation of business relationships. Every interaction with customers, employees, contractors, vendors, and partners should be documented with proper agreements. This chapter covers the essential contracts you need to operate CoodLoom legally and protect your interests.

Why Contracts Matter

The Cost of No Contract

Scenario 1: The Unpaid Customer

  • Customer uses CoodLoom for 6 months
  • Stops paying, claims software "doesn't work"
  • No written contract specifying terms
  • Result: Can't collect payment, $30K loss

Scenario 2: The Departing Contractor

  • Hired developer to build feature
  • No written contract
  • Developer claims they own the code
  • Demands $50K to transfer rights
  • Result: Pay ransom or rebuild feature

Scenario 3: The Scope Creep Customer

  • Agreed to build custom feature for $10K
  • No statement of work
  • Customer keeps requesting changes
  • "You promised this would be included!"
  • Result: Endless work, no additional payment

Prevention: Proper contracts for every business relationship

Types of Contracts CoodLoom Needs

Customer Contracts

1. Software License Agreement (SLA) 2. Terms of Service (ToS) 3. Service Level Agreement (SLA) 4. Statement of Work (SOW) - for custom work 5. Master Services Agreement (MSA)

Employment Contracts

6. Offer Letter 7. Employment Agreement 8. Contractor Agreement 9. Advisory Agreement 10. Confidentiality Agreement (NDA)

Business Contracts

11. Vendor Agreements 12. Partnership Agreements 13. Reseller Agreements 14. Non-Disclosure Agreements

Other Important Docs

SaaS vs Licensed Software

SaaS Model (Recommended for CoodLoom):

  • Customer accesses via web/cloud
  • No installation on customer premises
  • Subscription-based
  • Continuous updates
  • Easier to enforce (cut off access if non-payment)

Licensed Software:

  • Customer installs on their servers
  • Perpetual license or term license
  • Customer controls updates
  • Harder to enforce compliance

Recommendation: SaaS model with subscription licensing

Should CoodLoom Offer SLA?

Pros:

  • Builds trust with enterprise customers
  • Competitive differentiation
  • Shows confidence in infrastructure
  • Required by many large customers

Cons:

  • Financial liability if breach
  • Need robust monitoring
  • Pressure to maintain high uptime
  • May limit maintenance windows

Recommendation:

  • Early stage: No formal SLA (or 99% informally)
  • With enterprise customers: 99.5-99.9% SLA
  • At scale: 99.9% or higher

Typical SaaS SLAs:

  • 99.9% (43.8 min downtime/month): Industry standard
  • 99.95% (22 min/month): Premium
  • 99.99% (4.4 min/month): Mission-critical
  • 99.999% (26 sec/month): Extremely expensive

Contract Negotiation Tips

Negotiating Customer Contracts

What to Push Back On:

  • Unlimited liability (always cap)
  • Indemnification for everything (limit scope)
  • Source code escrow (expensive, negotiate out)
  • Right to audit (limit frequency, notice period)
  • Exclusive rights (retain right to serve other customers)

What to Accept:

  • Reasonable security requirements
  • Standard confidentiality
  • Right to terminate for cause
  • Background checks for employees accessing their data

Red Flags:

  • "Perpetual, irrevocable license" (you can never terminate)
  • Personal guarantees (risk your personal assets)
  • Unreasonable indemnification (you're liable for everything)
  • Assignment of all IP (they own your improvements)

Standard Terms to Always Include

Every Contract Should Have: 1. Parties: Full legal names 2. Effective Date: When contract starts 3. Term: Duration (and auto-renewal if applicable) 4. Termination: How either party can end 5. Payment Terms: Amount, frequency, late fees 6. Warranties: What you promise (and disclaim) 7. Limitation of Liability: Cap on your exposure 8. Indemnification: Who pays if sued 9. Governing Law: Which state's laws apply 10. Dispute Resolution: Arbitration vs litigation 11. General Provisions: Entire agreement, severability, amendments 12. Signatures: Both parties sign and date
