Company Culture

Introduction

Culture isn't what you say—it's what you do. It's the behaviors you reward, the standards you enforce, and the examples you set. As founder of CoodLoom, you have a unique opportunity to intentionally build culture from day one. This chapter shows you how to create a culture that attracts top talent, drives performance, and scales with your company.

What Is Company Culture?

The Simple Definition

Culture = The Way We Do Things Here It's:

  • How decisions are made
  • What behaviors are rewarded
  • How people treat each other
  • What's celebrated vs what's punished
  • How conflicts are resolved
  • What success looks like

Culture Is Not

❌ Ping pong tables and free snacks
                        
❌ Mission statement on the wall ❌ What you say in all-hands meetings ❌ Company values document ❌ Perks and benefits ❌ Fun team events These can reflect culture, but they ARE NOT culture.

Culture Is

✓ Engineer admits mistake in front of team → Praised for transparency
                        
✓ Sales person lies to customer → Immediately fired ✓ Someone works 80 hours → Asked why systems failed, not praised ✓ Junior person disagrees with CEO → Their opinion is genuinely considered ✓ Customer issue arises → Multiple people jump to help without being asked ✓ Team member struggling → Colleagues proactively offer support These behaviors = your actual culture

Defining Your Culture

Step 1: Define Core Values

What Are Values? Core beliefs that guide behavior and decisions. Bad Values (Meaningless):

❌ Innovation (too vague)
                        
❌ Excellence (everyone claims this) ❌ Teamwork (says nothing specific) ❌ Integrity (baseline expectation) ❌ Customer-focused (of course you are)
Good Values (Specific, Behavioral):

✓ "Customer Obsession: We make decisions that might hurt short-term 
                        
metrics if they're right for customers long-term" ✓ "Radical Transparency: We share information openly, including mistakes and challenges, even when it's uncomfortable" ✓ "Bias to Action: We make fast decisions with incomplete data and iterate, rather than endlessly planning" ✓ "Own Your Outcomes: We take responsibility for results, not just effort. No blame, only learning" ✓ "Raise the Bar: Every hire should raise the average talent level. We're building a team of A+ players"

Answer these questions:

What does success look like for a person here? From your answers, extract 4-6 core values that:

                        
    
                        
  • Are specific and behavioral
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Guide difficult decisions
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Reflect your beliefs
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Differentiate you
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Will scale as you grow

Building Culture Daily

The Founder's Role

You Are the Culture: Everything you do is watched and copied:

  • When you arrive and leave
  • How you handle stress
  • How you treat people
  • What you celebrate
  • What you tolerate
  • How you make decisions
  • How you communicate

Example Scenarios:

Scenario: Customer complains about bug
                        
Your response option 1: "Ugh, users don't understand how to use it" → Team learns: Blame customers Your response option 2: "Interesting, let's understand what they were trying to do and how we can make it clearer" → Team learns: Customer obsession Scenario: Engineer makes expensive mistake Your response option 1: "How could you let this happen?!" → Team learns: Hide mistakes, fear taking risks Your response option 2: "Thanks for catching and fixing it. What did we learn? How do we prevent this systematically?" → Team learns: Transparency and learning Scenario: Someone working 80 hours/week Your response option 1: Praise them publicly as "most dedicated" → Team learns: Overwork is expected Your response option 2: "Why are you working so much? Do we have a planning or staffing problem? Let's fix the system" → Team learns: Work-life balance is valued

Assessing Culture in Interviews

Value-Based Interview Questions: For "Customer Obsession":

"Tell me about a time you went above and beyond for a customer 
                        
or user, even though it wasn't your job." Listen for: ✓ Specific example with clear customer impact ✓ Taking initiative without being asked ✓ Empathy for customer's situation ✓ Following through to resolution Red flags: ❌ Can't think of an example ❌ Blames customers for problems ❌ Only did the minimum required
For "Radical Transparency":

"Describe a time you had to admit a mistake to your team or manager. 
                        
What happened?" Listen for: ✓ Took ownership quickly ✓ Explained what they learned ✓ Didn't make excuses ✓ Took action to prevent recurrence Red flags: ❌ Blames others ❌ Minimizes the mistake ❌ Doesn't mention learnings
For "Bias to Action":

"Tell me about a time you made a decision without having all the 
                        
information you wanted. What was the outcome?" Listen for: ✓ Moved quickly when needed ✓ Used available data ✓ Set up feedback loops ✓ Learned and iterated Red flags: ❌ Analysis paralysis ❌ Waited for perfection ❌ Blamed lack of data
The Culture Interview:

Dedicated 30-45 min interview focused on values:
                        
1. Introduce CoodLoom values (5 min) 2. Ask 2 questions per value (20 min) 3. Candidate asks about culture (10 min) 4. Wrap up (5 min) Score each value: Strong Fit, Fit, Weak Fit, No Fit Any "No Fit" = Don't hire, regardless of skills

Red Flags

Cultural Red Flags (Don't hire):

❌ Badmouths previous employer excessively
                        
❌ Takes credit for team work ❌ Blames others for failures ❌ Disrespectful to receptionist/coordinator ❌ Doesn't ask questions about culture ❌ Only talks about money/title ❌ Rigid "this is the only way" thinking ❌ Can't admit mistakes or weaknesses ❌ Late or unprepared for interview ❌ Talks over interviewer
Green Flags (Hire):

✓ Talks about team success, not just personal
                        
✓ Specific examples of learning from failure ✓ Asks thoughtful questions about culture ✓ Shows genuine curiosity ✓ Respectful to everyone ✓ Excited about mission and vision ✓ Self-aware about strengths and growth areas ✓ Evidence of continuous learning ✓ Comfortable with ambiguity ✓ Coachable and open to feedback

Common Culture Killers

1. Tolerating Brilliant Jerks

The Trap:

"Yes, they're difficult, but they're so talented/productive!"
                        
Impact:

                        
    
                        
  • Good people leave
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Culture erodes
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Team becomes toxic
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Collaboration breaks down
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Innovation decreases

The Solution:

Netflix's approach: "Adequate performance + Great culture fit" 
                        
beats "Brilliant performance + Culture destroyer" Fire the brilliant jerk. Every time. Quickly. The team will celebrate (internally or openly).
How to Handle:

1. Have direct conversation
                        
"Your work is excellent, but your behavior is damaging the team. Here are specific examples. This must change." 2. Give clear expectations and timeline "In the next 30 days, I need to see [specific behaviors]" 3. Follow up weekly 4. If no change, let them go "Despite your technical skills, your behavior doesn't align with our values. Today is your last day." 5. Tell the team why (without trashing them) "We parted ways because of cultural misalignment. We value collaboration and respect above all, even technical excellence."

2. Values-Actions Mismatch

The Trap:

Say: "We value work-life balance"
                        
Do: Praise people working weekends Say: "We value transparency" Do: Make decisions behind closed doors Say: "We value diversity" Do: Hire people all from same background Team sees: You don't mean what you say
The Solution:

Audit your stated values vs actual behaviors:
                        
    
                        
  • What do you reward?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • What do you punish?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • How do you spend time?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • What behaviors do leaders model?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
If there's mismatch: Either change the values or change the behaviors.

3. Growing Too Fast

The Trap:

Pressure to grow quickly → Lower hiring bar → Wrong people → 
                        
Culture dilutes → Good people leave → More pressure to hire → Even lower bar → Death spiral
The Solution:

Grow as fast as you can hire A+ players, no faster.
                        
Better to:

                        
    
                        
  • Turn down customers
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Delay features
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Grow slower
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Miss revenue targets
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Than to:

                        
    
                        
  • Hire wrong people
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Destroy culture
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Lose great team members
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Build unsustainable company

4. Founder Disconnect

The Trap:

Early: Founder knows everyone, models culture daily
                        
Later: Founder in meetings all day, team rarely sees them Result: Culture drift, team feels disconnected
The Solution:

Schedule Regular:
                        
    
                        
  • Lunch with random team member (weekly)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • "Office hours" (anyone can book 15 min)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Working sessions (code/design/sales with team)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • All-hands Q&A (don't skip)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Respond in Slack (be visible)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Attend team events (not above it)
    • 
                        
                        

                        
You set the culture. You must be present.

5. Ignoring Early Warning Signs

Warning Signs:

🚨 Increased turnover (especially of good people)
                        
🚨 Negative Glassdoor reviews 🚨 Difficulty hiring 🚨 Lack of energy in all-hands 🚨 Complaints about "politics" 🚨 Information silos forming 🚨 Decreased collaboration 🚨 More conflict, less resolution 🚨 Low engagement survey scores 🚨 People stop speaking up
The Solution:

Address immediately:
                        
1. Acknowledge there's an issue 2. Gather data (surveys, 1-on-1s, exit interviews) 3. Identify root causes 4. Create action plan 5. Communicate plan transparently 6. Execute and follow up 7. Measure improvement Don't wait. Culture problems compound.

The Exit Interview

When Someone Leaves (Even if fired):

Ask (in person or video, not email):
                        
1. What did you like most about working here? 2. What frustrated you most? 3. Did you feel your contributions were valued? 4. How was your relationship with your manager? 5. What would have made you stay? 6. What should we change? 7. Would you work here again in the future? 8. Would you recommend us to others? Listen without defending. Thank them for honesty. Take notes. Look for patterns across exits.

□ Hire for culture add, not just culture fit
                        
□ Train managers on culture reinforcement □ Expand cultural rituals □ Create culture FAQ □ Share culture externally (blog, social) □ Review and evolve values (if needed) □ Measure: retention, eNPS, hiring speed

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Culture = behaviors, not words: What you do and reward, not what you say
  • ✓ You are the culture: As founder, your actions set the tone
  • ✓ Values must be specific: Vague values are useless; make them behavioral
  • ✓ Hire for culture add: Values alignment + diversity of thought
  • ✓ Fire brilliant jerks: Protecting culture > short-term performance
  • ✓ Systematize early: Don't wait until 50+ people
  • ✓ Rituals reinforce culture: Repeated behaviors become norms
  • ✓ Recognize and reward: What gets recognized gets repeated
  • ✓ Measure continuously: Use quantitative and qualitative data
  • ✓ Evolution is normal: Culture should evolve, but values should stay stable
