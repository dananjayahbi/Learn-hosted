Vision Setting

Introduction

Your vision is the North Star for CoodLoom. It's where you're going, why it matters, and how the world will be different when you get there. A compelling vision attracts top talent, inspires teams through hard times, focuses decision-making, and ultimately determines what you build. This chapter teaches you how to create, articulate, and evolve a vision that turns CoodLoom from an idea into an empire.

Understanding Vision

What Is Vision?

Vision: A clear, compelling picture of the future you're creating. Not just:

❌ "We want to be successful"
                        
❌ "We'll build great products" ❌ "We aim to make money"
But rather:

✓ "We're making AI content creation effortless for every business"
                        
✓ "We're building the operating system for modern content teams" ✓ "We're creating a world where anyone can produce professional content without technical skills"

Vision vs Mission vs Values vs Strategy

The Hierarchy:

Vision (Where we're going):
                        
"In 10 years, CoodLoom will be..." ↓ Mission (Why we exist today): "We exist to solve [problem] for [customers]" ↓ Values (How we behave): "We believe in [principles] and act with [behaviors]" ↓ Strategy (How we'll get there): "We'll achieve vision by [approach]" ↓ Goals/OKRs (What we'll do this quarter): "In Q1, we'll accomplish [specific objectives]"
Example for CoodLoom:

Vision (10-year):
                        
"Become the world's most trusted AI platform for content creation, powering 1 million businesses" Mission (Current): "Empower businesses to create exceptional content effortlessly using AI that understands their unique voice and goals" Values:

                        
    
                        
  • Customer obsession
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Innovation with purpose
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Radical transparency
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Quality over quantity
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Continuous learning
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Strategy (3-year): "Focus on SMB market first, then move upmarket to enterprise. Build best-in-class AI engine, then expand to adjacent workflows." Goals (This quarter): "Reach $100k MRR, launch v2.0, hire 5 people, achieve <5% churn"

Creating Your Vision

The Vision Creation Process

Step 1: Start with Why Simon Sinek's Golden Circle:

Why (Purpose):
                        
"Why does CoodLoom exist? Why do you do what you do?" How (Process): "How do you do it differently?" What (Product): "What do you build/sell?" Most companies communicate outside-in (What → How → Why) Great companies communicate inside-out (Why → How → What)
For CoodLoom:

Why:
                        
"We believe every business deserves to have a powerful content presence, but creating quality content is too hard, time-consuming, and expensive. We're democratizing professional content creation." How: "By building AI that truly understands your brand voice, goals, and audience, and can create content that feels authentically yours." What: "An AI-powered content creation platform that helps businesses produce blog posts, social media, marketing copy, and more."
Your Why Exercise:

Answer these questions:
                        
1. Why did you start CoodLoom? 2. What problem in the world bothers you? 3. What change do you want to see? 4. What would you work on even if you weren't getting paid? 5. What do you want your legacy to be? Your answers contain your "why."
Step 2: Paint the Future 10-Year Vision Exercise:

It's 2035. CoodLoom has succeeded beyond your wildest dreams.
                        
Describe:

  • How has the world changed because CoodLoom exists?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • What are people saying about CoodLoom?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • What does your company look like?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • What are you most proud of?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • What problems have you solved?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Who have you impacted?
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Be specific. Be ambitious. Be inspiring.
Example:

"In 2035, CoodLoom powers content creation for 1 million businesses 
                        
worldwide. Marketing teams that used to spend 20 hours per week on content creation now spend 2 hours, freeing them to focus on strategy and creativity. Small businesses that couldn't afford content teams now produce content that rivals Fortune 500 companies. We've democratized professional content creation, just like Shopify democratized e-commerce and Stripe democratized payments. CoodLoom is known as the most trusted AI platform—not just for quality, but for transparency, ethics, and putting humans first. We've proven that AI can amplify human creativity rather than replace it. Our team of 500 people works remotely from 30 countries, united by our mission to empower every business with great content. We're profitable, growing sustainably, and have created massive value for customers, employees, and shareholders."
Step 3: Make It Memorable The Elevator Pitch Version: Take your 10-year vision and distill to one sentence. Formula options: "We're building [product] to [outcome] for [audience]"

Example:
                        
"We're building the AI content platform that makes professional content creation effortless for every business"
"A world where [vision of future]"

Example:

"We're the [position] for [category]"

Example:

Requirements for Good Vision Statement:

✓ Inspirational (makes people excited)
                        
✓ Clear (anyone can understand) ✓ Specific (not generic platitudes) ✓ Ambitious (stretches possibility) ✓ Believable (audacious but achievable) ✓ Memorable (people can repeat it)

Vision Components

Your Vision Should Include: 1. Scale/Ambition:

Numbers show ambition:
                        
❌ "We want to help businesses" ✓ "We'll serve 1 million businesses" ❌ "We'll be successful" ✓ "We'll be the category leader"
2. Impact:

How is the world different?
                        
❌ "We'll build great software" ✓ "We'll democratize content creation, enabling small businesses to compete with enterprises"
3. Timeline:

When does this happen?
                        
"In 10 years..." "By 2035..." Gives people sense of journey.
4. Unique Position:

What makes you different?
                        
❌ "We'll be an AI company" ✓ "We'll be the most human-centric AI company" ❌ "We'll have customers" ✓ "We'll be the trusted brand in AI content"
The Full Vision Framework for CoodLoom:

Aspirational Vision (10 years):
                        
"By 2035, CoodLoom will power content creation for 1 million businesses worldwide, democratizing professional content and proving that AI can amplify human creativity." Mission (Now): "We empower businesses to create exceptional content effortlessly using AI that understands their unique voice and goals." Strategic Position: "We're building the trusted AI platform for content teams—known for quality, transparency, and putting humans first." Core Impact: "We're creating a world where every business, regardless of size or budget, can produce content that drives real results."

Communicating Your Vision

The Vision Story

Structure:

Part 1: The Problem (30 seconds)
                        
Paint the pain. Make them feel it. "Every business knows content marketing drives growth. But creating quality content is brutal—it's time-consuming, expensive, and requires specialized skills most small businesses don't have. As a result, 80% of small businesses can't compete with the content produced by enterprises with full marketing teams." Part 2: The World You're Creating (30 seconds) Share your vision. "We're building a world where that changes. Where a 5-person startup can produce content that rivals a Fortune 500 company. Where marketing teams spend their time on strategy and creativity, not repetitive content creation. Where AI amplifies human creativity rather than replacing it." Part 3: How You'll Get There (30 seconds) Your strategy. "CoodLoom's AI learns your brand voice, understands your goals, and creates content that feels authentically yours. We're starting with blog posts and social media for SMBs, then expanding to enterprise content operations. In 10 years, we'll power content for 1 million businesses worldwide." Part 4: The Call to Action (15 seconds) What you want from them. "We're looking for [investors who believe in this vision / engineers who want to build the future of content / customers ready to transform their content strategy]. Join us in democratizing content creation." Total: ~2 minutes

Evolving Your Vision

When to Evolve Vision

Vision Should Be Stable (Mostly):

Your core vision—the big picture of what you're creating—should 
                        
remain relatively stable for years. But strategy, tactics, and specific goals will change as you learn.
When Vision Should Evolve: Market Shifts:

Example:
                        
Original: "We're building AI content for blogs" Market shift: Businesses need AI for all content types Updated: "We're building the AI platform for all content creation"
Achieved Initial Vision:

Example:
                        
Original: "Serve 10,000 SMBs" Achievement: You're at 15,000 SMBs Updated: "Serve 100,000 businesses across all sizes"
New Insights:

Example:
                        
Original: "Make content creation faster" Learning: Speed matters less than quality and brand voice Updated: "Make creating on-brand, quality content effortless"
Pivot:

Sometimes you need to completely change direction.
                        
This is rare but happens. When pivoting:

                        
    
                        
  • Communicate why clearly
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Show what you learned
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Paint new vision compellingly
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Get team buy-in
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Move decisively

Vision in Practice

Using Vision for Decision-Making

The Vision Filter:

For every major decision, ask:
                        
"Does this move us toward our vision?" If yes → Consider it If no → Probably not worth doing If unclear → Discuss and clarify
Examples: Product Decision:

Question: Should we build enterprise features or improve SMB product?
                        
Vision: "Democratize content creation for small businesses" Answer: Improve SMB product first. Enterprise can wait. (Vision guides the decision)
Hiring Decision:

Question: Hire experienced executive or scrappy operator?
                        
Vision: "Move fast and serve SMBs" Answer: Scrappy operator who understands SMBs. (Vision guides the hire)
Partnership Decision:

Question: Partner with established player or build in-house?
                        
Vision: "Best-in-class AI engine" Answer: Build in-house for control and quality. (Vision guides the approach)

Common Vision Mistakes

Mistake #1: Too Vague

❌ "We want to make the world better"
                        
❌ "We'll build great products" ❌ "We'll be successful" These could apply to anyone. ✓ "We're democratizing professional content creation for 1 million small businesses by 2035" Specific. Measurable. Unique.

Mistake #2: Too Small

❌ "We'll have 100 customers"
                        
❌ "We'll make a small profit" ❌ "We'll be a nice little business" Not inspiring. Doesn't attract top talent or investors. ✓ "We'll be the category-defining AI platform for content teams" Ambitious. Exciting. Worth dedicating years of life to.

Mistake #3: Too Unrealistic

❌ "We'll replace Google in 2 years"
                        
❌ "Every human on Earth will use our product" ❌ "We'll solve all content problems instantly" No one believes it. Loses credibility. ✓ "We'll serve 1 million businesses in 10 years" Audacious but believable with execution.

Mistake #4: Copying Others

❌ "We'll be the [famous company] of [our industry]"
                        
Derivative. Not inspiring. Shows lack of original thinking. ✓ Define your own category and vision. "We're not trying to be the next anyone. We're building something that hasn't existed before."

Mistake #5: Never Communicating It

You have a vision in your head, but:
                        
    
                        
  • Team doesn't know it
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Customers don't hear it
    • 
                        
                        

                        
    
                        
  • Investors don't understand it
    • 
                        
                        

                        
Vision in your head ≠ Vision that drives company Communicate it relentlessly.

Mistake #6: Changing It Too Often

Month 1: "We're building X for Y"
                        
Month 2: "Actually, we're building Z for W" Month 3: "No wait, we're building A for B" Creates confusion, lack of trust, no momentum. Vision should be stable for years (with minor refinements). Strategy and tactics can change, vision shouldn't (usually).

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Vision is your North Star: Everything else cascades from it
  • ✓ Start with why: Purpose drives everything
  • ✓ Make it specific: Vague visions don't inspire
  • ✓ Make it ambitious: Think 10x, not 10%
  • ✓ Make it memorable: One sentence people can repeat
  • ✓ Communicate relentlessly: Rule of 7—they need to hear it constantly
  • ✓ Connect to daily work: Show how every task advances vision
  • ✓ Use it for decisions: Vision is your decision-making filter
  • ✓ Review regularly: Evolve as you learn, but keep core stable
  • ✓ Live it yourself: Your belief must be genuine and visible

  • Call to action (15s)

Record yourself. Watch it. Improve.

Exercise 3: Vision Cascade

Create the full cascade:

  • 10-year vision
  • 3-year strategic goals
  • 1-year goals
  • This quarter's OKRs
  • This week's priorities

Show how each connects.

Exercise 4: Communication Plan

Create plan to communicate vision:

  • Daily touchpoints
  • Weekly mentions
  • Monthly deep dives
  • Quarterly reviews

Schedule and execute.

Exercise 5: Vision Audit

  • Do customers understand where you're going?
  • Do decisions reference vision?
  • Is vision visible everywhere?

Score yourself. Improve weak areas.
